About Treevance

Founded in 2010 by a team of passionate arborists, Treevance has grown from a small local service to the premier tree care provider in the Northeast. Our name reflects our mission - combining Tree innovation with forward-thinking Advancements in arboriculture. We've expanded our services to cover all aspects of tree care while maintaining our commitment to sustainable practices and scientific methodology.

What sets us apart is our commitment to evidence-based tree care. All our arborists maintain ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) certification and participate in ongoing education. We invest in the latest diagnostic technology including resistographs, sonic tomography, and air spade tools to provide accurate assessments. Our research division collaborates with universities to develop new techniques for urban tree care.

Our municipal division has managed urban forestry programs for over 30 communities, helping them develop sustainable canopy management strategies, respond to invasive pests like the spotted lanternfly and emerald ash borer, and maximize the environmental benefits of their urban forests. We've helped municipalities secure over $2 million in grants for tree planting and maintenance programs.

For residential clients, we offer a full suite of services from routine maintenance to emergency storm response. We're proud to be the tree care provider of choice for many historic properties and conservation areas, helping preserve specimens that are centuries old. Our residential clients benefit from the same level of expertise we bring to municipal contracts, ensuring healthy, beautiful trees that enhance property values and quality of life.

Tree Care Tips

Proper tree care is essential for maintaining healthy trees and enhancing the beauty of your landscape. Here are some expert tips to help you care for your trees:

  • Regularly inspect your trees for signs of disease or damage.
  • Water deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth.
  • Mulch around the base to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
  • Prune during the dormant season to promote healthy growth.
  • Consult with a certified arborist for any major tree work.
  • Fertilize your trees in early spring to provide essential nutrients.
  • Monitor for pests and diseases, and act quickly to mitigate issues.
  • Consider planting native species that are well-adapted to your local environment.
  • Ensure proper spacing between trees to allow for healthy growth and airflow.
  • Educate yourself about the specific needs of the tree species you have.
